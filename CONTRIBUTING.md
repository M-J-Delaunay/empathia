# 🤝 Contributing to Empathia

> *"Are you empathetic?"*

Welcome. The fact that you are reading this means something already passed between you and this idea. We are glad you are here.

Empathia is built by the community, for the world. There is no corporation behind it. No investors. No agenda other than the one written in the README.

This document explains how to join the journey.

---

## 💜 Before Anything Else

Before contributing code, design, or ideas — read the [README](README.md).

Not because it is a rule. Because everything we build here flows from that vision. If the vision resonates with you, you are already one of us.

Ask yourself one question before every contribution:

> *"Does this serve empathy — or does it serve engagement?"*

If the answer is empathy, welcome.

---

## 🌱 Who Can Contribute

Everyone. Always.

- **Developers** — frontend, backend, mobile, security, infrastructure
- **Designers** — UI, UX, accessibility, visual identity
- **Translators** — every language brings new humanity to Empathia
- **Lawyers & jurists** — help us build the legal vision for the future
- **Writers** — documentation, manifesto, community guidelines
- **Thinkers** — philosophers, psychologists, educators
- **Anyone** who believes empathy can be a measure of civilization

There is no seniority here. The first contributor in 2026 has the same dignity as the one who arrives in 2034.

---

## 🚀 How to Contribute

### 1. Report an issue or share an idea
Open a **GitHub Issue** and describe:
- What you observed or imagined
- Why it matters for empathy
- Any suggestion you have

There are no bad ideas here. Only unexplored ones.

### 2. Propose a change (Pull Request)
- Fork the repository
- Create a branch with a clear name (`feature/empathy-score-display`)
- Make your changes
- Submit a Pull Request with a clear description of what and why

### 3. Translate
Open an Issue titled `Translation: [language]` and we will coordinate together.

### 4. Design
Share mockups, wireframes, or design systems via an Issue or by email. No tool is required — a sketch photographed on paper is welcome.

## 🌍 Code of Conduct

Empathia is a space where empathy is not just the product — it is the method.

We ask every contributor to:

- **Listen before responding** — understand before you react
- **Disagree with kindness** — conflict is welcome, contempt is not
- **Welcome the newcomer** — every expert was once a beginner
- **Give credit generously** — acknowledge the work of others
- **Accept imperfection** — we are building something new, together

Toxic behavior will be addressed by the community, not by an algorithm.

---

## 🏆 Recognition

Every contributor — however small their contribution — will be:

- Listed on the **Contributors page**
- Eligible to receive **community donations** as an act of gratitude
- Free to **remove their name** at any time

No contribution is too small. A single translated sentence has reached someone who would otherwise have been alone.

---

## 🔄 The Technical Decisions

Empathia does not impose a technical stack. The README suggests a starting point. The community decides what is best.

If you believe a different technology serves the vision better — open an Issue and make the case. We build by consensus, not by decree.
